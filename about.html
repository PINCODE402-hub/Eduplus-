Our Story

Two decades of keeping one promise

Founded in memory of a schoolteacher who believed discipline and warmth could live in the same classroom.

How We Began

One classroom, one conviction

Daniel Memorial Academy takes its name from Mr. Daniel Kweku Mensah, a village schoolteacher known for two things in equal measure: how firmly he held his students to a standard, and how gently he noticed when one of them was struggling at home.

After his passing, his family and former students pooled resources to open a single Creche classroom in his name — determined that his approach to teaching shouldn't end with him. Twenty-four years later, that one classroom has grown into a full campus carrying children from their very first day of school through to JHS graduation.

The name on the gate has stayed the same. So has the conviction behind it.

What Guides Us

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To provide every child, from Creche through JHS 3, with a rigorous academic foundation and a steady moral compass — delivered by teachers who know each student by name, not just by grade.

Our Vision

To be the school families trust with their child's entire basic education journey — known as much for the character of our graduates as for their results.

Our Values

Four things we never compromise on

Academic Rigor

Real accountability — regular assessment, and results parents can see and understand.

Pastoral Care

Every class has a homeroom teacher who knows each child's story, not just their grade sheet.

Safety First

A gated, supervised campus with sign-in/sign-out procedures parents rely on daily.

Beyond the Classroom

Sports, music, drama and clubs — because character grows outside the syllabus too.
